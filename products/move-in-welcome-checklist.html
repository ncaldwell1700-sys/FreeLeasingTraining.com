Move-In Welcome Packet Checklist

Resident:    |  Move-in date:    |  Free Leasing Training template

Before move-in day

  • Mid-gap check-in sent (between signing and move-in) confirming date and any prep needed
  • Unit walked personally to confirm cleaning and any promised repairs/upgrades are complete
  • Keys, fobs, and access codes ready and tested
  • Move-in paperwork prepared (lease copy, parking permit, any required forms)
  • Utility setup instructions ready to hand over (what's included vs. resident's responsibility)

Information to include in the welcome packet

  • How to submit a maintenance request (and expected response time)
  • Trash, recycling, and parking rules/locations
  • Mailbox/package locker location and access instructions
  • Office hours and best way to reach the team
  • Wifi/internet setup info, if applicable
  • Local tips: nearest grocery store, transit stop, or other quick orientation notes

On move-in day

  • Someone available in person (or by phone) to hand over keys and answer questions
  • Walked the resident through anything non-obvious (thermostat, appliances, building entry)
  • A small welcome gesture included (note, small gift, or personal "welcome home" message)

First two weeks

  • Follow-up check-in sent (call, text, or email) asking how the move-in went
  • Any issues raised during check-in logged and followed up on:  
A resident's experience between signing and move-in shapes how they feel about the entire property — including whether they renew. The leasing relationship doesn't end at signing; it just changes phase.
