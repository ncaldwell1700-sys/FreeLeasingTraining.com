Tour Prep Checklist

Unit / property:    |  Date:    |  Free Leasing Training template

Before the prospect arrives

  • Walked the unit in person within the last 24 hours — not relying on memory or photos
  • Lights on in every room, blinds/curtains open
  • No maintenance tape, leftover supplies, or turnover debris visible
  • Climate set to a comfortable temperature before arrival
  • Confirmed the unit is actually the one being shown (not double-booked or already leased)
  • Reviewed what the prospect told you when they inquired (commute, pets, work-from-home, etc.)
  • Have current pricing, availability date, and fee details ready without needing to look them up live

What you know about this specific prospect

  • What they said matters most:  
  • Any objection or hesitation mentioned on the phone/by email:  
  • Who's deciding (just them, a partner, a roommate?):  

During the tour

  • Asked at least one open question before starting the walkthrough
  • Highlighted the 1-2 features that match what this prospect said mattered to them
  • Addressed any visible imperfection directly rather than ignoring it
  • Mentioned current move-in specials or fee waivers, if applicable
  • Asked directly what they thought before the tour ended

Before they leave

  • Confirmed a clear next step (application, second tour, a specific follow-up time)
  • Provided a way to reach you with questions
  • Noted anything to follow up on afterward:  
A tour that's prepped for the specific prospect — not just the unit — converts at a higher rate than a generic walkthrough. Five minutes of prep before they arrive is the highest-leverage five minutes in the whole leasing process.
