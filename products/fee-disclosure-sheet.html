How to use this sheet

  1. Fill in the blanks for your specific property/unit before your first tour of the day — don't try to recall numbers from memory mid-conversation.
  2. Hand this to the prospect (physically or as a PDF) at the end of the tour, before they ask "so what's the total cost?" Being the one to bring it up signals transparency.
  3. Walk through each line briefly out loud — don't just hand it over silently. This is also your chance to mention any current move-in specials or fee waivers that apply.
  4. Keep a digital copy ready to email immediately after the tour, so the prospect has it in writing to share with a partner/roommate or reference later.
  5. Update this sheet any time pricing, fees, or policies change — an outdated fee sheet creates the exact trust problem this tool is meant to prevent.

Why this matters

Most application drop-off at the "I'm ready to apply" stage isn't about price — it's about an unexpected fee discovered partway through the application. Prospects who see every cost clearly, in writing, before they apply are far more likely to follow through, because there's nothing left to discover that could make them hesitate.
