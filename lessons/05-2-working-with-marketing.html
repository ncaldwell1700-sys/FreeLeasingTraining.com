Working with your marketing team

9 min

Leasing agents and marketing teams often work in separate worlds — marketing drives traffic and leads, agents convert them — but the two functions succeed or fail together. An agent who treats marketing as a black box that "just sends leads" is missing one of the easiest ways to improve their own numbers.

You have information marketing doesn't

You're the one actually talking to prospects. You hear, firsthand, what made someone inquire, what almost stopped them from touring, and what objections come up again and again. Marketing usually doesn't have direct access to any of that — unless you share it.

If you keep noticing prospects mention a specific listing photo, a specific price point concern, or a specific competitor they're comparing you to, tell marketing. That feedback can directly improve the quality of leads coming in next.

Lead quality is a two-way conversation

If you're consistently getting inquiries that don't match the property — wrong budget range, wrong unit type, wrong expectations — that's worth flagging specifically, not just complaining that "leads have been bad lately." Specific feedback ("we're getting a lot of inquiries expecting pricing closer to $X") is something marketing can actually act on; vague frustration isn't.

Marketing efforts need your follow-through to work

A great ad campaign or listing push is wasted if inquiries sit unanswered or tours go poorly once people show up. From marketing's perspective, your performance is part of what makes their work pay off — slow response times or weak tours can make a genuinely good lead source look like it's underperforming, when the issue is actually downstream.

Share what's working, not just what's broken

It's easy to only reach out to marketing when something's wrong. Sharing positive signals too — a listing photo prospects keep commenting on, a description that seems to resonate — helps marketing double down on what's actually working, not just react to complaints.

Treat it as one team, one outcome

Ultimately, marketing and leasing are optimizing for the same number: signed leases. Approaching the relationship as collaborative, rather than as "they send leads, I close them," tends to produce better leads, better conversations, and a property that performs better overall.
