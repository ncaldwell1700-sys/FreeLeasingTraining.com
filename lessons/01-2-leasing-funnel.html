The leasing funnel — how prospects become residents

10 min

In the last lesson, we said leasing is sales, not customer service — and that means managing a funnel, not just answering questions. Let's break that funnel down stage by stage, so you always know exactly what you're trying to accomplish.

Stage 1: Inquiry

A prospect calls, emails, texts, or fills out a form on a listing site. At this stage, your only goal is to get them excited enough to book a tour. You are not trying to close them over the phone — you're trying to earn 30 minutes of their time in person (or on a video call).

Stage 2: Tour

This is where most leases are actually won or lost. The tour isn't a walkthrough of square footage — it's where the prospect decides whether they can picture themselves living there. Your job is to listen for what they actually care about (commute, space, pets, price) and tailor what you show and say around that.

Stage 3: Application

A prospect who tours and likes the unit doesn't always apply on the spot. This stage is about removing friction — making sure they understand the process, the cost, the timeline, and that there's nothing confusing standing between "I like this place" and "I've submitted my application."

Stage 4: Signed lease

The application gets approved and the lease gets signed. Your job here is mostly logistics — but it's also your last chance to set expectations clearly so the move-in goes smoothly and the resident starts the relationship on a good note.

Why this matters

Every stage has a different goal. Treating a phone inquiry like a closing conversation — or treating a tour like a Q&A session instead of an experience — is why prospects drop out of the funnel. Know which stage you're in, and know what "success" looks like at that specific stage.

What's next
