Unit 2 · The prospect journey

Following up without being annoying

8 min

Most prospects don't apply the moment they leave a tour — they're comparing options, talking to a roommate or partner, or just need a day to think. Follow-up is where a lot of leases actually get won, but it's also where agents most often get it wrong by either disappearing or overdoing it.

Follow up the same day, every time

A short message within a few hours of the tour — thanking them for their time and offering to answer any questions — keeps you top of mind while the property is still fresh. Waiting two or three days to follow up means you're competing with whatever else they toured in between.

Make every follow-up add something new

The fastest way to feel "annoying" is sending the same message repeatedly: "just checking in!" with nothing new attached. Instead, give each follow-up a reason to exist — answer a question they raised on the tour, mention a unit that opened up, or share a deadline (like a move-in special ending) that's genuinely relevant to their decision.

Match their pace, don't force yours

If a prospect says they need a week to decide, respect that — but don't go silent for the full week either. A brief, low-pressure check-in mid-way through ("just wanted to see if anything came up that I could help answer") shows you're available without pushing.

Know when to stop

Not every prospect converts, and persistent follow-up past a clear "no" or prolonged silence stops being diligence and starts being a problem — for them and for your reputation. A good rule of thumb: two or three thoughtful follow-ups over one to two weeks is reasonable. Beyond that without any response, let it go and move your energy to active prospects.

Use the channel they responded on

If someone originally reached out by text, follow up by text — not a phone call out of nowhere. Matching their preferred channel respects how they want to communicate and gets a better response rate than switching to whatever's most convenient for you.
