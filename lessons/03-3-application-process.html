The application process — walking prospects through it

9 min

Getting a prospect to say "yes, I want to apply" feels like the finish line — but the application itself is still part of the funnel, and it's a stage where good prospects can stall out if it's not handled well. This lesson covers how to walk someone through it smoothly.

Explain before they start, not after they're confused

Before sending an application link, walk through what to expect: what documentation is needed (ID, proof of income, references), what the fee covers, and roughly how long approval takes. A prospect who knows what's coming is far less likely to abandon the process partway through when they hit a question they weren't expecting.

Be upfront about every cost

Application fees, deposits, and any other charges should be stated clearly and early — not discovered partway through the form. Surprise costs are one of the fastest ways to lose trust with someone who was otherwise ready to commit. If there's a process for fee waivers or specials, mention it proactively if it applies to them.

Make documentation easy

Not every prospect has their pay stubs or ID photo ready to go the moment they decide to apply. Tell them exactly what's needed in a simple list they can reference, rather than letting them discover requirements one at a time inside the application itself.

Stay reachable during the process

Applications can raise unexpected questions — about a co-signer, about income documentation, about timing. Make sure the prospect knows how to reach you if something comes up mid-application, and respond quickly. A prospect stuck on a confusing step with no easy way to ask a question is a prospect at risk of giving up.

Set expectations for what happens next

Once submitted, tell them what happens and when: "You'll typically hear back within 2-3 business days," or whatever your actual timeline is. Silence after submission creates anxiety and opens the door for a prospect to start looking elsewhere just in case.

Follow through on approval — fast

Once an application is approved, don't let momentum die. Reach out promptly with next steps for signing the lease. The excitement and certainty a prospect feels right after approval is the best moment to move quickly — don't let a slow handoff undercut it.
