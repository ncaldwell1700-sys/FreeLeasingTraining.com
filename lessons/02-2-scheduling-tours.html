Scheduling and confirming tours

7 min

Booking a tour is only half the job — a booked tour that doesn't show up is worth nothing. No-shows are one of the most common (and most avoidable) leaks in the leasing funnel. This lesson covers how to schedule tours that actually happen.

Offer specific times, not open-ended availability

"What time works for you?" creates work for the prospect and gives them an easy reason to put off deciding. Instead, offer two or three concrete options: "I have 11am or 2pm tomorrow — which works better?" Specific choices get booked faster than open questions.

Confirm in writing, even after a phone booking

If a tour is booked over the phone, follow up immediately with a text or email confirming the date, time, and address. This does two things: it gives the prospect something to reference later, and it creates a natural moment for them to flag a conflict before it becomes a no-show.

Send a reminder before the tour

A short reminder the day before (or morning of) significantly reduces no-shows — people forget, double-book, or simply lose track of plans made days earlier. Keep it brief and friendly: "Looking forward to showing you the place tomorrow at 2pm! Let me know if anything changes."

Make rescheduling easy, not awkward

Some agents treat a reschedule request as a bad sign and let the relationship go cold. Treat it as completely normal — life happens. Respond quickly, offer new times right away, and keep the same energy as the first conversation. A prospect who reschedules once is still a real prospect.

Know what you're confirming for yourself, too

Before the tour, make sure you know the unit's current condition, that it's actually ready to show, and that you have any answers the prospect is likely to need (pricing, availability dates, pet policy specifics) without having to look them up live. Nothing undercuts a tour faster than fumbling for basic information in front of the prospect.
